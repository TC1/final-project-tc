MARTINEZ vs ZIMMER

ALCS 2003

Game 3 of the 2003 ALCS between the Yankees and Red Sox was one of the more memorable in the series—but for reasons other than good baseball.

Pictured: Not Good Baseball.

The tumultuous day began after outfielder Karim Garcia was drilled in the back by a Pedro Martinez fastball. The Yanks retaliated in the bottom half of the inning with a high and inside pitch to the always feisty Manny Ramiriez.

Ramirez charged and a bench clearing brawl broke out. In the chaos, Don Zimmer was tossed to the ground by Martinez—igniting even more side fights and bickering. Don Zimmer who was 72 years old at the time might I add.

Well who didn't see that coming.

Roger Clemens would pitch well for the remainder of the game as the Yankees took Game 3, 4-3.

Zimmer later took more precautions when in Boston.
