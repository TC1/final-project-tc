HOOPS

Way back in 1891 a bunch of gym students at the Springfield (Massachusetts) College YMCA were bored with having to do gymnastics and calesthenics all winter long. Faculty tasked profressor James Naismith to develop a new game that was "Fair for all players and not too rough." Naismith sketched out some rules, nailed two peach baskets at opposite ends of the floor and dubbed the game Basketball. It was a huge success and Naismith went on to becoming one of the earliest top coaches of the game and lived to see Basketball become a part of the Olympics (1936) and the NCAA DI Tournament and Championship (1939)
